About

Welcome to the Legal Project Management Consortium 'Delivery Principles' website. The purpose of these pages is to share principles that unlock "legal project management" excellence and best-practice. You can view the principles here; measure of success are available here.

Our hope is that these principles will be adopted by firms, clients, lawyers and project managers. By standardising the way we deliver legal services, the way service providers and recipients work together can be enhanced, enabling commercial efficiency and higher quality outcomes.

Principles of Legal Project Management

Processes shows you how to "Do" vs. Principle guides you on how to "Be": understanding how to "Be" can be powerful: principles can guide us in any circumstance

The principles outlined below help to form the foundation for project management excellence in the legal industry. They were created and endorsed by a group of Legal Project Management though leaders, as a part of the Legal Project Management Consortium.

Principle 1

The highest priority is to satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery of valuable outcomes

Principle 2

All parties must work together transparently: communicate regularly throughout the full project lifecycle

Principle 3

The most effective method of conveying information is through frequent conversation

Principle 4

Welcome scope changes, even late in delivery

Principle 5

Focus on "Simplicity": deliver value while minimising complexity

(e.g. a simple delivery plan and fee structure)

Principle 6

Proactively minimise the 8 Lean Wastes*

TIMWOODS: Transport, Inventory, Motion, Waiting, Overprocessing, Overproduction, Defects, Skills,

Principle 7

A key measure of progress is "delivered work": create a continuous flow of value, minimise work-in-progress

Principle 8

Continuously improve in an empirical way (Kaizen)

Principle 9

The best products emerge from motivated self-organizing teams

Principle 10

Agile frameworks, methodologies and processes promote sustainable delivery, while minimising risk

Measures of Sucess

To continuously improve the way legal services are delivered, performance needs to be measure in a simple yet powerful way. The "Measures of Success" shown below, can help lawyers and clients rate project management quality.

This list is intended to be a guide, recognising that there is no "one-size-fits-all" solution appropriate for all matter types. Use this as a guide for metrics suitable for your own situation/environment.
Matter Planning

Matter Planning:
Check #1

A consolidated plan of all future deliverables is always available

Matter Planning:
Check #2

An overarching matter plan is actively used to manage all 3rd party activities



Financial Planning

Financial Planning:
Check #1

There is always a complete and up-to-date financial plan available

Financial Planning:
Check #2

The financial plan contains all invoiced costs and expected future costs (monthly granularity)



Commercial Efficiency

Commercial Efficiency:
Check #1

Commercial optimisations are established (e.g. fee optimisation structures) to provide cost saves

Commercial Efficiency:
Check #2

Optimisations are actively monitored to ensure they are effective



Operational Support

Operational Support:
Check #1

POs are actively managed (monthly)

Operational Support:
Check #2

Invoices are typically approved or rejected on the day of receipt

Operational Support:
Check #3

Payment issues are identified and resolved within a week

About the Creators

The team recognised that the legal industry needed to evolve project management capabilities, and partnered to define the principles and measures presented on this website.
